Reflection on the readings I selected, relating it to my experience with internet today: The readings I engaged with were: What Hypertext is by Noah Wardrip-Fruin and As we may think by Vannevar Bush. Regarding What Hypertext is, I grasped a basic understanding of what hypertext is using a definition by Marie Laure Ryan, “In hypertext… the reader determines the unfolding of the text by clicking on certain areas, the so-called hyperlinks, that bring to the screen other segments of text.” This definition got me to understand how interactivity deals with links, how one component is connected to another component, for example how the link to a published scholarly essay will direct you to a website that contains the PDF version of the essay where users can view and read the text. The reading also got me to learn about hypermedia which are branches and presentations which respond to user actions and how they are systems of prearranged words and pictures, which can be freely explored. The thing that caught my attention in As we may think, was how the text focused on the anticipation and vision of the technological devices we have today. It made me understand the limitations that people had before these advancements, for example how people had to store information in books, in organized and systematic ways that were time consuming and inefficient compared to the rapid technology we share today that allows users to efficiently locate information from vast sets of data. Instead of going to the library, we can access a digital library through a website such as Google Scholar, to be able to read the same information that is present in physical books. The reading also allowed me to understand the concept of Memex, although I don’t have a solid understanding of the concept, I understand how it is essentially a private file or library for individual use, it allows users to store various materials such as records and personal notes and offers them easy access to it. I think of Memex as a house for the user, where they can place their information in different rooms of the house and arrange them in any way they want. Interesting things I found about the history of the web: The thing I found interesting was how outdated devices such as dry photography and microphotography were once the most reliable and efficient devices to capture and store information. I was fascinated by how the passage As we may think, envisioned most of the devices we have today, for example a system where researchers can talk directly to a machine that types their spoken words, this just made me think of Siri and how this virtual assistant, collects the information of the user and presents it in a convenient way to the user, for example: “Hey Siri, what does interactivity mean?: and Siri will respond by reading the most common and popular definition on the internet. This made me appreciate the technology we have, because it was a vision for someone and a reality for us. Reflect on interaction and the www: what does interaction mean to you? Let me start of by mentioning my favourite part about the lecture: “we are in the business of manipulation”. I never thought of how as web designers, we manipulate the user to make certain actions such as clicking on a link on our websites, it made me think of the psychological part of it, and how we should understand our user to ultimately manipulate them. Interaction to me is how input leads to output. In order for something to be interactable there has to be an outcome as a result of an input. In web design, I think the interaction can be seen when the user clicks on a user interface element (home button) and it directs the user to the respective page (home page). The lecture taught me more about interactivity and how it should be responsive and not respond in a completely random fashion. I don’t think I’ve ever thought of how websites are not random, how when you click on the close button, it closes the tab and not shut down the computer. I also feel like a huge part of interactivity is ‘feedback’, because feedback allows the user to know that something has happened from something they did, an example of this would be how the light switches on once the laptop charger is switched on.
