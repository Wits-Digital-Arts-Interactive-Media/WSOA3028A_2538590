Reflect on Moulthrop, S. (2003) ‘You Say You Want a Revolution? Hypertext and the Laws of Media (1991)’: how does it speak to the internet today?

Moulthrop seemed to be concerned about the rapid growth of hypertext and how overwhelming it may be. He also reflects on the cyclical nature of technological trends and how hypertext could be temporarily popular. This made me think how technology is rapidly growing and how one technological advancement could be so popular but be replaced by other technological advancement, it makes me question whether they’ll ever be a technological advancement that cannot be surpassed in or if this will be a never ending cycle of constant technological improvements and ideas.

The reading also taught me how Hypertext systems utilize the interactive potential of computers to create variable-access databases, allowing any unit of discourse to have multiple associations.

Reflect on interaction and the www: what does interaction mean to you?

Let me start off by mentioning my favorite part about the lecture: “we are in the business of manipulation”. I never thought of how as web designers, we manipulate the user to make certain actions such as clicking on a link on our websites, it made me think of the psychological part of it, and how we should understand our user to ultimately manipulate them.

Interaction to me is how input leads to output. In order for something to be interactable there has to be an outcome as a result of an input. In web design, I think the interaction can be seen when the user clicks on a user interface element (home button) and it directs the user to the respective page (home page).

The lecture taught me more about interactivity and how it should be responsive and not respond in a completely random fashion. I don’t think I’ve ever thought of how websites are not random, how when you click on the close button, it closes the tab and not shut down the computer.

I also feel like a huge part of interactivity is ‘feedback’, because feedback allows the user to know that something has happened from something they did, an example of this would be how the light switches on once the laptop charger is switched on.
